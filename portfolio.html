Research portfolio

Selected projects aligned to your current “Research Highlights” themes: confined 1D materials, picoperovskites, radial vdW heterostructures, in-situ device characterisation, and templated thermoelectrics.

Confined halide perovskites (“picoperovskites”)

Confined halide perovskites and composite 1D heterostructures enabling tunable optoelectronic behaviour at the nanoscale.

Key points

  • Atomic-scale TEM/STEM + spectroscopy to resolve structure/defects in confined motifs.
  • Confinement/encapsulation used as a stabilisation and property-engineering lever.

Related: “Picoperovskites…” (Advanced Materials, 2023).

Confined extreme nanowires

Encapsulation of technologically important materials in ultranarrow templates to form 1D atomic chains and motifs with tunable properties and enhanced stability.

Key points

  • Template-directed formation of 1D chains/motifs otherwise unstable in bulk.
  • Atomic-resolution imaging to connect motif geometry to properties.

1D radial van der Waals heterostructures

CNT/BN/MoS₂ radial heterostructures supporting coexisting excitons and highly mobile charge carriers, enabling ultrafast optoelectronics at atomic scale.

Key points

  • Radial stacking to co-locate optical excitation and carrier transport channels.
  • Correlative structural/optical measurements to interpret ultrafast response.

Related: Nano Letters (2020) “Ultrafast Optoelectronic Processes in 1D Radial vdW Heterostructures”.

In-situ microscopy of phase-change devices

Elemental mapping of nonvolatile GeSbTe memory cells and selectors, linked to device-scale electrical behaviour for improved reliability and scaling.

Key points

  • Compositional mapping and structural evolution under relevant conditions.
  • Evidence linked back to device behaviour and scaling constraints.

Related: Faraday Discussions (2019) “Towards a 3D GeSbTe Phase Change Memory…”
