Overview

I am an experimental physicist and materials scientist specialising in advanced electron microscopy and the structure–property relationships of nanomaterials for energy and optoelectronic applications. My work combines high-resolution TEM/STEM and spectroscopy with optical and electrical measurements to understand how atomic-scale structure, defects and interfaces control device-relevant performance.

I have led research on confined halide perovskites (“picoperovskites”) in carbon nanotubes, 1D and 2D van der Waals heterostructures, and phase-change and thermoelectric materials, with a recurring theme of encapsulation/confinement to stabilise fragile phases and engineer optical/electronic response.

Research focus

  • Atomic-scale electron microscopy (TEM/STEM, EELS) of functional materials
  • Confined halide perovskites and “picoperovskite” nanostructures
  • 1D radial and 2D van der Waals heterostructures
  • Energy materials and optoelectronic devices (PV, photodetectors, memory)
  • Correlative structure–optics–transport measurements in working devices

Research highlights

Confined extreme nanowires

Encapsulation in ultranarrow templates to form 1D atomic chains and motifs with tunable properties.

Confined halide perovskites

Composite 1D heterostructures enabling tunable optoelectronic behaviour at the nanoscale.

1D radial vdW heterostructures

CNT/BN/MoS₂ radial stacks supporting coexisting excitons and mobile charge carriers.

In-situ phase-change devices

Elemental mapping of GeSbTe memory cells linked to device-scale electrical behaviour.
